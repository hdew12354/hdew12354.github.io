Kinect Dress-Up

'"you can only lie about who you are for so long without going crazy.” ~ Ellen Wittlinger, Parrotfish '

So, for this project, i wanted to make something for the Xbox Kinect, you can pick them up for about £20 on Amazon, and I thought to myself 'hey, i wonder what these can actually do?' and as such i decided hey, let's see how i can use this to try to bodge together some vr motion tracking!

only problem with that is, well, that solution kinda sorta already existed, and meshing with steamVR wasn't really working when i tried to work with it and eeh i dropped that idea and went for something a little more in my area of expertise! # i decided to make a Unity Game, but for what you may be asking? well, little known fact about me is, i'm trans, so i thought that i would make something to help with that problem!

What you'll need to run this TechDemo!................(The download for the techdemo is at the bottom of the page if ya just wanna speed through fam)

  • Xbox 360 Kinect
  • Windows PC/Laptop (Mac/Linux builds coming soon!)
  • Kinect for Windows SDK v1.8
  • Xbox 360 Kinect Power Supply (because the kinect has a weird non-standard connector, whereas the PSU has a usb connection)

What can This TechDemo Currently do

Well, This TechDemo currently has 2 models in it, one of which being the girl model which you can see on this page, and another being a guy model, both of which are nto mine, they were sourced from the asset store. this TechDemo is also compatible with the Kinect Studio, which can be used to create Motion capture data, allowing for creators to track their movements and see the model move in real time allowing them to correct their movements in relation to the model, so that their arms do not clip through the model

What CAN'T This TechDemo do (YET)

This TechDemo is planned to have more models, a model Selection menu, and an Interactivity ModelSet, which will allow for users to grab models in the scene and record the movements of those models.

This TechDemo is also planned to have Virtual Reality Support, so that users can experience life AS the models, which can help to alleviate dysphoria, and can also be quite cool even if you don't experience dysphoria!
